DevTrack v3.0 is out. The headline: it writes your standup update from your git commits and runs entirely on your machine — no account, no cloud, no server to babysit.

## What's New in v3.0

- **Fully Standalone:** Generates standup updates from git commits entirely offline. Zero cloud dependencies for the core workflow.
- **The Boardroom:** AI personas — architect, security reviewer, devil's advocate — stress-test your plans before you commit to them. Useful when you're the only technical person in the room.
- **Optional AI Backend:** For work-updates, PM sync, and reports — plug in if you want cloud features, skip it if you don't.
- **Plain-English Sprint Planning:** Describe what you're building in natural language; DevTrack turns it into structured sprint tasks.
- **Telegram Bot:** Full ticket and update management from your phone.

## Commitment

This is a daily-driver tool, not a weekend project. I use it every working day. That's the commitment to long-term maintenance — it has to work for me before I can ask it to work for you.

YouTube tutorial videos are coming next month, covering setup and the most common workflows.
